Distracted Driving

Understanding the dangers and making responsible choices

Safety Video

Key Points

Distracted driving is any activity that diverts attention from the primary task of driving. For CDL drivers, the stakes are significantly higher — a loaded tractor-trailer can weigh up to 80,000 lbs and takes much longer to stop than a passenger vehicle.

Under FMCSA regulations, commercial drivers are prohibited from texting or using a hand-held mobile device while operating a commercial motor vehicle. Violations can result in fines, disqualification, and out-of-service orders.

The three main types of distraction are: visual (taking your eyes off the road), manual (taking your hands off the wheel), and cognitive (taking your mind off driving). Texting combines all three.
